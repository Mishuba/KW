1.) In a face-to-face closing, who may be present?
    - Representative for the lending institution 
    - Represaentative for the title insurance company 
    - Buyer and seller 
        * Real estate professinoals representing the buyer and seller and attorneys for the buyer and seller may also be present. In a face-to-face closing, the parties may be meeting for the first time. 

2.) At closing, an item prepaid by the seller is 
    - Credited to the seller. 
        * Prepaid items are expenses that have been paid by seller but are not fully used up, such as fuel oil. These items are prorated and appear as a credit to the seller. 

3.) The process by which expenses are divided at settlement of a real estate transaction so taht both the buyer and the seller pay their respective portions of property charges is called
    - Proration 
        * The division of financial responsibility between the buyer and the seller for property charges is called proration. 

4.) All encumbrances and liens shown on the report of title, other than those waived or agreed to by the purchaser and listed in the contract, must be removed wso that the title can be delivered free and clear. The removal of such encumbrances is typically the duty of 
    - The seller 
        * Removal of encumbrance is the (financial) responsibility of the seller. 

5.) The document that provides the borrower with general information about settlement costs, RESPA provisions, and what happens at settlement is 
    - Your Home Loan Toolkit 
        * It provides numerous resources for further information as well as a line-by-line description of the Loan Disclosure form. 

6.) The TILA-RESAPA Integrated Disclosure rule (TRID) requires that 
    - The borrower be given an estimate of the closing costs before the time of the closing. 
        * TRID requires that the lender provide the borrower with the Loan Estimate no more than three business days after receiving the loan application. The Closing Disclosure must be provided no later than three business days before the date of closing. 
        
7.) All of the following items are usually prorated between the buyer and the seller at closing EXCEPT 
    - Recording Charges 
        * The seller usually pays for recording charges necessary to clear all defectgs and furnish the purchaser with a marketable title. The purchaser pays for recording charges that arise from the actual transfer of title. 

8.) The Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act (RESPA) dapplies in a loan assupmtion if 
    - the terms of the assumed loan are modified by the lender. 
        * If the terms of the assumed loan are modified or the lender charges more than $50 for the assumption, then the transaction is subject to RESPA regulations. 

9.) At the closing of a real estate transaction, the person performing the settlement gave the buyer a credit for certain accrued items. These items were 
    - Bills relating to the property that the buyer must pay. 
        * Accured items are those property-related expenses (bills) that remain on the property as it is sold and that the purchasers will have to pay later. 

10.) A buyer purchases a home in an area where closings are traditionally conducted in escrow. Which item would a buyer deposit with the escrow agent before the closing date? 
    - Cash needed to complete the purchase 
        * The buyer is normally responsible for depositing the cash needed to complete the transaction. 

11.) In some parts of the country, closing is called 
    - Settlement and transfer 
        * Closing procedures vary somewhat in different parts of the country; in some areas, itis called settlement and transfer. 

12.) Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act (RESPA) regulations apply to first-lien mortgage loans made to finance the purchase of 
    - one-to-four-family homes, cooperatives, and condominiums. 
        * RESPA regulations apply to first-lien mortgage loans made to finance the purchase of one-to-four-family homes, cooperatives, and condominiums.

    - A credit to the buyer. 
        * The purchaser's earnest (deposit) money is usually shown in a contract of sale as credited toward closing expenses and, if there is enough, toward any down payment.

    - An affiliated business arrangement
